<![CDATA[# Idea Brief Template

Use this template when you are starting from a raw concept: a product idea, a game, a tool, a workflow, an automation, a problem you want to solve, or any other starting point that is not yet implementation-ready.

The Idea Brief is the first artifact in the ForgeOS pipeline. It captures what you are trying to build and why, without requiring you to know how. It is meant to be messy, incomplete, and honest about what you don't know yet.

---

Copy everything below this line into `docs/IDEA.md` in your project:

---

```markdown
# Idea Brief

## What is it?
[Describe the thing you want to build. A product, a game, a tool, an automation,
a solution to a problem, a workflow improvement. Plain language. No jargon required.]

## Who is it for?
[Who will use this? Be specific if you can, vague if you can't yet.
"Developers who manage multiple repos" is better than "everyone."]

## What problem does it solve?
[Why does this need to exist? What is painful, slow, broken, or missing today?]

## Why now?
[Why build this now instead of later? Is there a trigger, a deadline,
a market shift, a personal itch? If the answer is "I just want to," that counts.]

## What does success look like?
[Describe outcomes, not features. "Users can do X without needing Y"
is better than "build a dashboard with three tabs."]

## What do you already know?
[Constraints, preferences, technical choices, prior art, things you've tried.
Anything that narrows the solution space.]

## What don't you know yet?
- [Open question 1]
- [Open question 2]
- [Open question 3]

## Related resources
[Links, screenshots, competitor products, inspiration, napkin sketches.
Anything that helps an AI tool understand what you're going for.]
```

---

## What happens next

Once you have filled in enough of the Idea Brief to draw boundaries around what the system does and does not do, create `docs/SPEC.md`. The spec is where ambiguity turns into constraints.

You do not need to answer every question in the Idea Brief before moving to a spec. You just need enough clarity to say "this is in scope" and "this is not."

If you are using AI tools to help refine the idea, paste the Idea Brief into the conversation as context. The brief is the first contract between you and the AI: it tells the tool what you care about and what you don't know yet, so it can help you explore rather than assume.
